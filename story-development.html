Story Development

$1,000 (up to 10k words)

Those pesky ideas you keep thinking of. When inspiration strikes, you grab whatever's closest.

You've got fragments everywhere - Google Docs, planners, Scrivener files, even that stack of paper on your desk your pet likes to chew on. There’s a story in there.

You just need hep with the foundation so you can start building the house.

Send it all to me. I'll help you turn fragments, notes, and half-formed ideas into a cohesive, readable story framework. Together, we'll shape your characters, plot, pacing, and structure from the ground up so you're no longer staring at a blank page, but working from a solid foundation.

This is hands-on, collaborative story-building designed to give you a clear path forward.

What's Included

A narrative first draft (story framework)

You'll receive a serviceable first draft written in narrative form—the story happening on the page without dialogue or detailed setting prose.

Think of it as a detailed, readable story skeleton:

  • • Events unfold in order
  • • Characters make choices and react
  • • Turning points and emotional beats are clear
  • • Pacing and structure are visible

You'll be able to see how the story works then write the novel from there.

Up to 10,000 words of complete structural foundation with all major beats, turning points, and character arcs mapped out in narrative form.

Unlimited revisions at the structural stage

We refine the framework until it feels right. If something isn't working, we adjust. This is collaborative, iterative, and focused on getting the story structure solid before you move on to prose.

Ownership & flexibility

  • • All rights to the story remain entirely yours
  • • Works for standalone novels or series (pricing adjusted if scope expands)

Timeline

2–3 weeks for initial draft

Plus additional time for revisions (varies by complexity)

What Happens Next

Once your story framework is complete, you can:

  • • Write the full manuscript yourself
  • • Return for Beta Reading or Manuscript evaluation once the draft is finished
  • • Continue into full ghostwriting (Drafts 2 & 3), if desired

Best For Authors Who:

  • ✓ Have a strong concept but freeze at the blank page
  • ✓ Feel overwhelmed by scattered notes, outlines, and ideas
  • ✓ Want a clear structural map before writing prose
  • ✓ Write best once the "what happens" is figured out
  • ✓ Need help executing a story concept, taking a premise to a plot
