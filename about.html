Hi, I'm Tazia

I was born when my mother was pursuing a master's degree. To keep an eye on me, I was nestled in between mountains of books so she could concentrate on her thesis and prevent an untimely death at the hand of my siblings' stomping feet. She later said, in hindsight, it was not wise to place a baby in between 900 page monstrosities. Ah, we live and we learn.

In any case, I think that's where my love for words began. I read anything and everything I could get my hands on and my mother loved taking us bookshop browsing after school, letting us pick a book or two after each trip.

And, naturally, when the stories I wanted to read couldn't be found, I started writing my own.

In high school, I wrote three original plays that were put on by the Drama club during midyear recitals and won a couple of ribbons and trophies, currently gathering dust somewhere in a cardboard box.

In college, drowning under the course-load of my STEM degree, I joined a Creative Writing workshop that took place on Wednesday afternoons, rearranging my schedule so I could squeeze in a couple of hours a week writing guilt-free.

At the end of the semester, I left with a couple of contacts from my critique group and one of them reached out midway through the next, asking if I could take a look at her completed novella. She offered to pay handsomely and even though I like to tell myself I'd have done it for free, I agreed. And that was how it began.
