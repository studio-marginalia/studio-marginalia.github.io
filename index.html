What is a beta reader?

A beta reader is your story’s first audience. Someone who spots what works, what doesn’t, and most importantly, gives you the why.

Do I need a beta reader?

Well, is this you?

  • Afraid your book isn’t ready but can’t tell what’s wrong?
  • Worried about bad reviews or readers abandoning your story?
  • Can’t afford expensive editing but still want professional feedback?
  • Tired of conflicting or unhelpful feedback from other beta readers?
  • Feeling isolated in the writing process?

If any of these sound familiar, a beta reader can give you clarity, confidence and constructive feedback that helps your book shine.

Why should I hire you?

What You Get

  • Because my beta reading is so thorough, most clients skip developmental editing entirely - saving them $1,500-$3,000.
  • You'll get feedback that catches the issues making readers quit your book, with clear explanations on exactly how to fix them.
  • There's a difference between personal preference and objective story problems, and I don't blur the line.

My Approach

I read your manuscript as both a storyteller and a story-lover; spotting the plot holes that would confuse readers, the pacing issues that make people put books down, and the character moments that don't quite land.

But I'm not just hunting for problems.

I'm also your biggest champion, celebrating what's working brilliantly in your story and helping you see why readers will fall in love with your book.

Why It Works

Rather than just reading your manuscript, you're getting someone who experiences it like your future readers will.

You’ll get feedback across seven key story elements, with insights grounded in how writing techniques affect readers.

After years of doing this, clients come back with the same statement again and again - this beta read gave them everything they needed.

What is your process?

1: Getting to know your story

▼

I start by reading the first 3,000 words of your manuscript - completely free!

Think of it as a creative first date - we're both figuring out if we're a good match.

This helps me understand your story, writing style, and goals. Within a day, I will get back to you to let you know that I'm interested in your story and would love to work together!

2: Making it official

▼

If we're both ready and excited to collaborate, we'll move into the paperwork phase.

We’ll finalize a contract covering timelines, deliverables, confidentiality, and payment terms, using my template or yours.

Once signed, I'll send over an invoice.

3: Getting started.

▼

Once payment is received, share your manuscript with me via Google Docs or Word document.

If you have specific questions or areas to focus on, send them along. I’ll address them in a supplemental question bank alongside your main reader report.

4: Delivering your feedback.

▼

Within 15-20 days (depending on manuscript length), here's what you'll receive:

In-line comments: Your manuscript with margin notes showing my real-time thoughts and reactions as I read.

Reader Report: A comprehensive analysis with actionable feedback across seven story elements.

Your questions answered: If you've asked specific questions, I'll address each one with detailed responses based on my reading experience.

5: Follow-up call.

▼

Take a moment (or a day or five) to digest the feedback, then book a complimentary 30-minute call.

The time is yours, so use it however works best for you!

Want to discuss plot issues, brainstorm revisions, or gush about your characters - I’m here for all of it!

Availability

To give every manuscript the attention it deserves, I only take on three clients per month. This way, you know your story is getting my full focus, not squeezed between a dozen other projects.

Who Am I?

I started out on Wattpad during its golden years. Back then, the comment section was capped at 2,000 characters and my fellow 11-year-olds and I felt like literary gods.

What began as thoughtful comments on popular stories soon turned into the first project I ever published on the platform - a book where each chapter broke down a trending story and gave readers the verdict: binge or skip.

My little project took off. Soon, newcomers and veterans haunted my profile, hungry for my take on the next must-read.

But alas, at 14, I learned how to do winged eyeliner. Wattpad wasn't so cool anymore and I left my old stomping grounds behind.

Years later, in university, a friend asked me to read her novella. I devoured all 8,000 words in a day and sent her detailed feedback. She loved it.

And just like that, I was back.

What I Bring

Since rediscovering my passion, I've been beta reading professionally since 2021. I started as part of a collective of five beta readers. We were a reading dream team where clients got five different perspectives on their manuscript. It was intense, thorough, and writers loved having that many fresh eyes on their work.

When we eventually went our separate ways, I kept doing what I loved - reading and giving feedback.

My mission is simple: indie authors shouldn't have to pay thousands of dollars just to have someone help them create the best possible version of their work. That's why I've built my entire service around providing developmental-level feedback at beta reading prices.

At the end of the day, great stories shouldn't be held hostage by high price tags. Making quality feedback accessible is my commitment to you and the indie author community I'm proud to serve.

FAQ

Don't see your question here? Email at info.studiomarginalia@gmail.com

Will beta reading actually help my book sell better?

▼

A beta reader can't guarantee sales, but we can help you avoid the things that make readers abandon books: confusing plot holes, pacing that drags, characters who don't feel real.
When readers finish your book satisfied and eager to recommend it, that's when word-of-mouth happens. Beta reading ensures your story is engaging from start to finish, giving you a solid foundation for success.

Do I need a beta reader?

▼

Not every writer needs a beta reader but almost every writer benefits from having one. When you’ve been close to your story for so long, it’s easy to miss plot holes, pacing issues, or unclear character motivations.
A beta reader gives you a fresh perspective. We spot what works, what doesn’t, and how your story comes across to someone outside your own head.
Our feedback can save you time in revisions, strengthen your manuscript, and help you feel more confident before sharing your work with a wider audience.

Do I need a beta reader if I'm planning to hire a developmental editor?

▼

You might not need both! Many of my clients find my feedback comprehensive enough to skip developmental editing entirely, saving them $1,500-$3,000.

Can I see a sample of your work before committing?

▼

Absolutely! I read the first 3000 words of every manuscript completely free. It’s a chance to see my feedback style and determine if we’re a good fit.

Do you work with first-time authors?

▼

Yes! I love working with debut authors. My feedback is designed to be educational and encouraging, helping you understand not just what to fix but why.

What genres do you work with?

▼

I read across most fiction genres. During the sample read, I’ll let you know if your story and genre are a good match for my expertise.

What if my manuscript isn’t finished yet?

▼

I work with complete manuscripts only. Your story needs a beginning, middle, and end so I can properly assess pacing, character arcs, and overall story satisfaction.

How do I submit my manuscript?

▼

Once contracts and payment are complete, share your manuscript via Google Docs or Word document. Include any specific questions or focus areas for me to address.

How do you deliver feedback?

▼

You'll receive detailed feedback in multiple formats: margin notes throughout your manuscript capturing my real-time reactions as I read, a comprehensive Reader Report with actionable insights across seven story elements, plus answers to any specific questions you've submitted. We'll also schedule a complimentary 30-minute follow-up call to discuss everything.

Do you correct grammar or spelling?

▼

My focus is on the story, not proofreading line by line. That said, if glaring errors distract the reading experience, I’ll point them out.

What if I have specific concerns about my manuscript?

▼

Perfect! Send any questions or areas of focus with your submission. I’ll address them in a supplemental question bank alongside your main reader report.

What if I'm not satisfied with the feedback?

▼

I stand behind my work. If the feedback doesn’t meet the standards outlined in our contract, we’ll discuss how to make it right. The free sample read also helps prevent mismatched expectations.

How do I protect my unpublished work?

▼

Confidentiality is built into our contract. I never share or discuss your manuscript with anyone else—your story stays completely private.

Do you work with series or sequels?

▼

Yes! I love working with series. If you bring me your first book, we can work together through the entire series as you write it. If you find me later in the process (say, at book 3), I'll read the previous books for a small additional fee to get up to speed, then we can continue from there. This way I can give you consistent, informed feedback that considers your ongoing character arcs and world-building.

Can we discuss the feedback after I receive it?

▼

Absolutely! Use the complimentary 30-minute follow-up call to discuss plot issues, brainstorm revisions, or clarify any points in your feedback.
