Manuscript Critique

Craft-informed critique that reveals why your story works, where it loses momentum, and how to strengthen it

Every manuscript has blind spots.

And as writers, we're often too close to our own work to recognise what they are.

My role is to uncover those blind spots and provide a clear, objective assessment of your manuscript. I read your story like a reader, but critique it with an editor's eye. By combining authentic reading experience with craft-level analysis, I can show you not only what isn't working, but why it isn't working and how to strengthen it.

There's a difference between personal preference and objective story problems, and I don't blur the line, allowing you to approach revisions with confidence rather than second-guessing every decision.

However, this isn't just a hunt for problems. My goal is to be both coach and teammate. Beyond pointing out what's broken, I'm also your biggest cheerleader, showing you why readers will fall in love with what you've already built.

Who This Service Is For

A manuscript critique is ideal if:

  • You know something isn't working, but can't quite pinpoint what
  • You're unsure how your story will land with readers
  • You've received conflicting advice from critique partners or writing groups and need one clear, objective assessment
  • You're deep into revisions and could use an experienced second set of eyes to help you move forward
  • You're preparing to query, self-publish, or move on to professional editing
What You Receive

In-line Comments

For manuscript critique, the comments show my analysis in action. They highlight specific places where the manuscript’s structure, character work, pacing, clarity, or emotional impact are succeeding or could be strengthened. You’ll see exactly where those patterns appear in the story, while the Critique Letter explains how they connect across the manuscript.

Comprehensive Critique Letter

Following the read, you'll receive a detailed critique letter covering these core elements of your manuscript:

  • Plot

    Whether the story unfolds logically, maintains conflict and tension, and delivers a satisfying emotional payoff.

  • Characters

    Whether the characters feel believable and consistent, whether their motivations make sense, whether their roles serve the story, and how effectively they connect with the reader.

  • Setting/Worldbuilding

    Whether the world feels immersive, internally consistent, and strengthens the story rather than distracting from it.

  • Theme

    Whether the central ideas emerge naturally and resonate throughout the narrative.

  • Pacing

    How effectively the story maintains momentum, where it slows down, and where it may move too quickly.

  • Readability and Flow

    How smoothly the prose reads, whether scenes transition naturally, and how easily the narrative carries the reader forward.

  • Overall Reader Experience

    How all of the story's elements come together, what lingers after the final page, and the overall impression the manuscript leaves.

  • Genre & Reader Expectations

    Whether the manuscript delivers on the expectations of its intended genre, who the story is most likely to resonate with, and whether it reaches its target audience effectively.

My critique is further separated into two distinct sections:

What's Working

A strong critique explains not just what's working, but why it succeeds.

This section highlights the aspects of your story that stand out, why they resonate, and how they strengthen the overall reading experience.

Notes on Improvement

A strong critique doesn't just identify problems. It explains them.

This section identifies the areas where your story loses impact, explains why they're affecting the reading experience, and offers suggestions to help guide your revisions.

Communication & Support

This is a hybrid email and call service designed to keep the process as simple as possible.

You're welcome to email me with:

After Your Critique

One follow-up call (no time limit), where we can:

  • Discuss any aspect of the critique
  • Clarify questions or points of uncertainity
  • Brainstorm revision strategies
  • Talk through your manuscript and next steps

Scheduled at a time convenient for both of us.

90 Days of Revision Support

Revision is a process, and I don't intend to hang you out to dry.

For 90 days following your critique, you'll have ongoing email support while you work through your revisions. To ensure every response remains thoughtful and comprehensive, I reply to one revision email each week, so I encourage you to gather your questions into a single message whenever possible.
